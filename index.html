🧾 2026 年谁需要缴纳自雇税？

只要您的净自雇收入达到 400 美元或以上，就需要缴纳自雇税（SE Tax）——包括自由职业者、收取 1099-NEC 表格的独立承包商、网约车与零工司机、网店卖家，以及有副业收入的人士，即使您同时拥有一份 W-2 全职工作。

2026 年自雇税率为 15.3%：12.4% 社会保障税（适用于工资上限 $184,500 以内的收入）加 2.9% 医疗保险税，按净自雇收入的 92.35% 计征。收入超过 $200,000（单身）或 $250,000（已婚联合申报）后还需加征 0.9% 附加医保税。好消息是：一半的自雇税可以从收入中扣除——本计算器会自动应用这项扣除。

由于没有雇主代扣税款，自雇人士通常需用 1040-ES 表格按季度预缴预估税；满足安全港规则（Safe Harbor）可避免罚款。符合条件的纳税人还可享受 20% 的 QBI 合格经营收入扣除。下方表单已为您预设好自雇状态——只需填入自雇收入即可，深入解读请参阅我们的税务文章。

🧾 Who Owes Self-Employment Tax in 2026?

You owe self-employment (SE) tax when your net earnings from self-employment reach $400 or more — that covers freelancers, independent contractors paid on Form 1099-NEC, gig and rideshare drivers, online sellers, and anyone with a side business, even alongside a W-2 job.

The 2026 SE tax rate is 15.3%: 12.4% for Social Security on earnings up to the $184,500 wage base, plus 2.9% for Medicare. It applies to 92.35% of your net self-employment income, and an extra 0.9% Medicare surtax kicks in above $200,000 (single) or $250,000 (married filing jointly). Half of your SE tax is deductible from income — this calculator applies that deduction automatically.

Because no employer withholds for you, plan quarterly estimated payments with Form 1040-ES; meeting the safe-harbor rules avoids underpayment penalties. Eligible taxpayers can also claim the 20% QBI deduction. The form below is pre-configured for self-employment — just enter your SE income, and see our tax articles for the full guide.

📈 2026 年长期资本利得如何计税

持有股票、ETF、加密货币或房产等资产超过一年再出售，利润即属于长期资本利得，可享受联邦优惠税率；持有不足一年则为短期利得，按普通收入计税，税率最高可达 37%。

2026 年长期资本利得税率：应税收入在 $49,450（单身）、$98,900（已婚联合申报）或 $66,200（户主）以内的部分适用 0%；超过以上门槛适用 15%；收入一旦超过 $545,500 / $613,700 / $579,600 则适用 20%。资本利得叠加在普通收入之上计税，因此一笔大额卖出可能把部分利润推入更高档位。

高收入者超过 $200,000/$250,000 后可能还需缴纳 3.8% 净投资收入税（NIIT，本工具未建模）。税损收割（Tax-Loss Harvesting）可以抵减利得，但需遵守 30 天洗售（Wash-Sale）规则。请在下方长期资本利得（LTCG）栏中输入金额，即可立即查看适用税率。

📈 How Long-Term Capital Gains Are Taxed in 2026

Hold an asset — stocks, ETFs, crypto, real estate — for more than one year before selling, and your profit qualifies as a long-term capital gain with preferential federal rates. Sell within a year and the gain is short-term, taxed as ordinary income at rates up to 37%.

For 2026, the long-term rate is 0% up to taxable income of $49,450 (single), $98,900 (married filing jointly), or $66,200 (head of household); 15% above those thresholds; and 20% once income exceeds $545,500, $613,700, or $579,600 respectively. Gains stack on top of your ordinary income, so one large sale can push part of the profit into a higher capital-gains bracket.

High earners may also owe the 3.8% Net Investment Income Tax above $200,000/$250,000 (not modeled here). Tax-loss harvesting can offset gains — just respect the 30-day wash-sale rule. Enter your long-term gains in the LTCG field below to see your bracket instantly.

👶 2026 年儿童税收抵免：每位儿童最高 $2,200

2026 税年，每名年底未满 17 周岁的符合条件儿童，最多可为您带来 $2,200 的儿童税收抵免（CTC）。儿童必须是您的被抚养人，满足亲属关系与共同居住测试，并在报税截止日前拥有可用于就业的社会安全号（SSN）。

如果抵免额超过您的税额，可退还的附加儿童税收抵免（ACTC）最多还能为每名儿童退回 $1,700——即使您不欠税也能拿到退税。收入超过 $200,000（单身）或 $400,000（已婚联合申报）后抵免逐步取消：每超出 $1,000，抵免减少 $50。

本计算器在计算完税档税额后再应用抵免，您可以清楚看到每位儿童如何降低联邦税单。下方表单已预设为 2 名儿童——请按家庭实际情况调整人数。EITC 收入所得税抵免、子女照护抵免等其他家庭优惠暂未纳入计算。

👶 Child Tax Credit 2026: Up to $2,200 per Child

For 2026, the Child Tax Credit is worth up to $2,200 for each qualifying child under age 17 at year-end. The child must be your dependent, meet the relationship and residency tests, and have a Social Security number valid for employment issued before the return's due date.

If the credit exceeds your tax bill, the refundable Additional Child Tax Credit (ACTC) can put up to $1,700 per child back in your pocket as a refund — even if you owe nothing. The credit phases out once income passes $200,000 (single) or $400,000 (married filing jointly), shrinking by $50 for every $1,000 over the threshold.

This calculator applies the credit after computing your bracket tax, so you can see exactly how each child lowers your federal bill. The form below is pre-set to two children — adjust the count to match your family. Other family benefits such as the EITC and the dependent-care credit are not modeled here.

💼 FICA 税的用途与 2026 年缴费标准

FICA（联邦保险供款法）工资税用于资助社会保障（Social Security）和医疗保险（Medicare）。雇员需缴纳 6.2% 的社保税加 1.45% 的医保税，雇主等额匹配，合计为工资的 15.3%。

2026 年，6.2% 的社保税只适用于前 $184,500 的工资——超过工资上限的部分免征社保税。医保税没有上限：每一美元工资都按 1.45% 计征，工资超过 $200,000（单身）或 $250,000（已婚联合申报）后还需加征 0.9% 附加医保税。

自雇人士需要自己承担雇主和雇员两侧——即全部 15.3% 的自雇税；这种情况请使用我们的自雇税计算器。与所得税不同，FICA 没有标准扣除额和税档之分：从工资的第一美元起就开始征收。请在下方输入工资金额，查看您的 2026 年工资税。

💼 What FICA Taxes Fund — and What You Pay in 2026

FICA — the Federal Insurance Contributions Act — funds Social Security and Medicare. Employees pay 6.2% for Social Security plus 1.45% for Medicare, and your employer matches both, for a combined 15.3% of wages.

For 2026, the 6.2% Social Security tax applies only to the first $184,500 of wages — earnings above the wage base are free of Social Security tax. Medicare has no cap: every dollar is taxed at 1.45%, and an Additional Medicare Tax of 0.9% applies to wages above $200,000 (single) or $250,000 (married filing jointly).

Self-employed workers pay both sides — the full 15.3% — as self-employment tax; see our self-employment tax calculator for that scenario. Unlike income tax, FICA has no standard deduction or brackets: it starts at the first dollar of wages. Enter your wages below to see your 2026 payroll tax.

计算详解：联邦税是怎么算出来的How the Calculator WorksCómo Funciona la Calculadora

想知道「联邦税」这个数字是怎么算出来的吗？本计算器严格遵循 IRS Form 1040 的计算顺序，共 8 个步骤：

第 1 步：汇总总收入 (Gross Income)

总收入 = 工资 (W-2) + 自雇净收入 + 其他普通收入 + 长期资本利得。

第 2 步：计算调整后总收入 (AGI)

AGI = 总收入 − above-the-line 扣除（如传统 IRA 供款、学生贷款利息）− 自雇税的 50%。

第 3 步：确定应税收入 (Taxable Income)

应税收入 = AGI −（标准扣除 与 逐项扣除 两者中较大者）。2026 年标准扣除：单身 $16,100 / 已婚联合 $32,200 / 户主 $24,150。

第 4 步：普通收入按累进税率计税

普通应税收入（不含长期利得）按 10% 到 37% 的七级累进税率逐层计算，每一层只按自己的税率征税。

第 5 步：长期资本利得单独计税

长期利得「叠」在普通应税收入之上，按 0% / 15% / 20% 三档税率计算（2026 年单身 0% 档上限为 $49,450）。

第 6 步：减去儿童税收抵免 (CTC)

每名符合条件的儿童抵免 $2,200，其中最多 $1,700 可退还——税额可降至零以下，形成净退税。

第 7 步：计算 FICA / 自雇税

W-2 工资：6.2% 社保税（上限 $184,500）+ 1.45% 医保税（超过 $200,000 / $250,000 再加 0.9%）。自雇收入：按 92.35% 的基数征收 15.3%。

第 8 步：对比预扣税，得出退税或补税

总税额 = 所得税 + FICA / 自雇税。与全年预扣税 (withholding) 比较：预扣多则退税，不足则补税。有效税率 = 总税额 ÷ 总收入；边际税率 = 应税收入触及的最高档。

Ever wondered how the "federal tax" number is produced? This calculator follows the IRS Form 1040 order of operations in 8 steps:

Step 1: Total Your Gross Income

Gross income = W-2 wages + net self-employment income + other ordinary income + long-term capital gains.

Step 2: Compute Adjusted Gross Income (AGI)

AGI = gross income − above-the-line deductions (traditional IRA contributions, student-loan interest, and the like) − 50% of self-employment tax.

Step 3: Determine Taxable Income

Taxable income = AGI − the greater of the standard or itemized deduction. The 2026 standard deduction is $16,100 Single / $32,200 Married Filing Jointly / $24,150 Head of Household.

Step 4: Apply the Progressive Brackets to Ordinary Income

Ordinary taxable income (excluding long-term gains) is taxed layer by layer across the seven brackets from 10% to 37% — each layer pays only its own rate.

Step 5: Tax Long-Term Capital Gains Separately

Long-term gains stack on top of ordinary taxable income and are taxed at 0%, 15%, or 20% (for 2026, the Single 0% bracket tops out at $49,450).

Step 6: Subtract the Child Tax Credit

Each qualifying child removes $2,200 of tax, and up to $1,700 per child is refundable — the tax can drop below zero into a net refund.

Step 7: Add FICA / Self-Employment Tax

W-2 wages: 6.2% Social Security (up to $184,500) + 1.45% Medicare (+0.9% above $200,000 / $250,000). Self-employment income: 15.3% on a 92.35% base.

Step 8: Compare Against Withholding

Total tax = income tax + FICA / SE tax. Compare with your annual withholding: overpaid means a refund, underpaid means a balance due. Effective rate = total tax ÷ gross income; marginal rate = the highest bracket your taxable income reaches.

为什么我的有效税率比我的税档低？Why is my effective rate lower than my tax bracket?¿Por qué mi tasa efectiva es menor que mi tramo impositivo?

▼

美国联邦所得税是累进制的：每档税率只作用于落在该档内的那部分收入，而非全部收入。例如身处 22% 税档的单身纳税人，前 $12,400 应税收入仍按 10%、下一层按 12% 征税。有效税率是各档的加权平均，因此永远低于您的最高（边际）税档。

US federal income tax is progressive: each bracket rate applies only to the slice of income inside that bracket, not to your entire income. For example, a Single filer in the 22% bracket still pays 10% on the first $12,400 of taxable income and 12% on the next layer. Your effective rate is the blended average of all brackets, so it is always lower than your top (marginal) bracket.

Employees split FICA with their employer, each paying 7.65%. Self-employed individuals pay both sides: 12.4% Social Security (up to the $184,500 wage base in 2026) plus 2.9% Medicare, a combined 15.3% on 92.35% of net self-employment income. The dividing line is one year of ownership. Assets held one year or less produce short-term gains, taxed as ordinary income at your regular brackets (10% to 37%). Assets held longer than one year qualify for long-term rates of 0%, 15%, or 20%, depending on your taxable income. Long-term treatment is usually much cheaper, which is why the holding period matters so much.
