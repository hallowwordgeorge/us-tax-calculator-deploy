Child Tax Credit 2026: Rules, Amounts, and Phase-Outs Explained

Child Tax Credit 2026: Rules, Amounts, and Phase-Outs Explained

The Child Tax Credit (CTC) is one of the most valuable tax benefits for families with children. For 2026 it grows under the OBBBA, but the details matter. Here are the amounts, eligibility rules, and phase-out thresholds in one place.

The Basics

In 2026, each qualifying child is worth a $2,200 credit (up from $2,000). A qualifying child must be under 17 at the end of the tax year; be your son, daughter, stepchild, foster child, or an eligible relative; live with you for more than half the year; receive more than half of their support from you; and have a Social Security Number valid for work.

Refundable vs. Non-Refundable Portions

The credit first wipes out your tax bill down to zero (the non-refundable portion). If credit remains, the Additional Child Tax Credit (ACTC) can return up to $1,700 per child as a refund — even if you owe no tax at all. The refundable amount equals 15% of earned income above $2,500, capped at $1,700 per child.

Income Phase-Out Thresholds

Above $200,000 (Single) or $400,000 (Married Filing Jointly), the credit shrinks by $50 for every $1,000 — or part of $1,000 — over the threshold. Example: a joint filer earning $420,000 is $20,000 over, so the total credit drops by 20 × $50 = $1,000.

How the Calculator Applies the CTC

Enter the number of qualifying children and the tool subtracts $2,200 per child from your income tax, treating up to $1,700 per child as refundable — which is why larger families sometimes see a negative income tax (a net refund). Note that the calculator does not model the income phase-out, so high-income households should verify with a professional.

💡 Phase-out example: a single filer earning $215,000 with one child is $15,000 over the threshold, losing 15 × $50 = $750 and leaving a $1,450 credit. Near the threshold, extra 401(k) contributions can cut your tax and preserve the credit at the same time.

儿童税收抵免 (Child Tax Credit, CTC) 是有娃家庭最重要的税收福利之一。2026 年它在 OBBBA 下进一步上调，但规则细节不少。本文把金额、资格和逐步取消门槛一次讲清。

基本规则

2026 年，每位符合条件的儿童可享受 $2,200 的抵免（高于此前的 $2,000）。「符合条件的儿童」需满足：税年结束时未满 17 岁；是你的子女、继子女、养子女或符合条件的亲属；一年中超过一半时间与你同住；你提供其一半以上的生活费用；拥有可用于工作的社会安全号 (SSN)。

可退还与不可退还部分

CTC 先把你的税单降到零（不可退还部分）；如果抵免还有剩余，Additional Child Tax Credit (ACTC) 可以让每名儿童最多 $1,700 以退税形式返还——即使你不欠税也能拿到。可退还金额按劳动收入超过 $2,500 部分的 15% 计算，上限为每名儿童 $1,700。

收入逐步取消门槛

收入超过 $200,000（单身）或 $400,000（已婚联合）后，抵免开始递减：每超出 $1,000（不足 $1,000 也按 $1,000 计），总抵免减少 $50。例如 MFJ 夫妻收入 $420,000，超出 $20,000，总抵免减少 20 × $50 = $1,000。

计算器如何处理 CTC

在本计算器中输入符合条件的子女人数，工具会按每名儿童 $2,200 自动从所得税中扣减，并将每名儿童最多 $1,700 视为可退还——这就是为什么子女较多的家庭有时会看到负的所得税（即净退税）。请注意，计算器未建模收入逐步取消，高收入家庭请以专业意见为准。

💡 逐步取消示例：单身收入 $215,000、有 1 名子女，超出门槛 $15,000 → 抵免减少 15 × $50 = $750，实际可得 $1,450。收入接近门槛时，增加 401(k) 供款可能同时降低税负并保住抵免。
